1. Introduction

ChroMarx ("we", "our", or "us") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you use our Chrome extension.

By installing and using ChroMarx, you consent to the data practices described in this policy. If you do not agree with the terms of this privacy policy, please do not access or use the extension.

2. Information We Collect

ChroMarx collects and processes the following data:

  • Bookmark data: Titles, URLs, and organizational structure of your bookmarks
  • User preferences: Settings and customization options you select
  • Account information: If you choose to sign in, we collect your email address and account identifiers
  • Usage statistics: Anonymous data about how you use the extension (optional and can be disabled)
  • Device information: Browser type and version, operating system, and screen resolution

3. How We Use Your Information

We use the collected information for the following purposes:

  • To provide and maintain our extension's functionality
  • To personalize your experience
  • To improve our extension and develop new features
  • To analyze usage patterns and optimize performance
  • To communicate with you regarding updates or support
  • To detect, prevent, and address technical issues

4. Data Storage and Security

Your data is stored:

  • Locally on your device using Chrome's storage API
  • In your Chrome sync account (if sync is enabled)
  • In our secure cloud database (if you create an account and enable cloud storage)

We implement appropriate technical and organizational security measures to protect your personal information from accidental loss, unauthorized access, and improper use or disclosure.

5. Data Sharing and Third Parties

We do not sell, trade, or otherwise transfer your personal information to third parties. We may share your information with:

  • Service providers: Companies that provide services to help us deliver our extension (e.g., hosting providers)
  • Analytics partners: To help us understand how our extension is used
  • Legal requirements: If required by law, court order, or governmental regulation

Third-party services we use:

  • Google Identity Services: For user authentication
  • Google Cloud Services: For AI-powered features (optional)
  • PayPal: For processing payments (optional)
  • Supabase: For cloud data storage and synchronization

6. Extension Permissions

ChroMarx requires these permissions:

  • storage: To save your bookmarks and preferences
  • bookmarks: To access and manage your Chrome bookmarks
  • identity: For optional Google sign-in
  • tabs: To access current tab information for bookmarking
  • sidePanel: To display the extension in Chrome's side panel
  • history: (Optional) For analyzing browsing history if enabled

We only use these permissions for the purposes stated and never in ways that compromise your privacy. Each permission is essential for specific functionality, and the extension will not access any data beyond what is necessary for its operation.

7. Your Data Rights

You have the right to:

  • Access your personal data
  • Correct inaccurate or incomplete data
  • Request deletion of your data
  • Export your data in a portable format
  • Restrict or object to certain processing of your data
  • Withdraw consent for optional data collection

To exercise these rights, visit the Settings page within the extension or contact us using the information below.

8. Data Retention

We retain your personal information only for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes outlined in this privacy policy, comply with legal obligations, or enforce our agreements.

When you uninstall the extension, local data is automatically removed. Cloud data (if enabled) is retained for 30 days after account deletion to allow for recovery, after which it is permanently deleted.

9. Children's Privacy

ChroMarx is not directed to children under 13 years of age. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If we discover that a child under 13 has provided us with personal information, we will promptly delete it.

10. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update our Privacy Policy from time to time. We will notify you of any changes by posting the new Privacy Policy on this page and updating the "Last Updated" date.

You are advised to review this Privacy Policy periodically for any changes. Changes to this Privacy Policy are effective when they are posted on this page.

11. Chrome Web Store Compliance

ChroMarx complies with the Chrome Web Store Developer Program Policies and the Google Chrome Terms of Service.

In accordance with these policies:

  • We do not display advertisements without clear notification to users
  • We do not use misleading installation tactics
  • We do not modify user search settings or homepage without explicit consent
  • We maintain transparency regarding the collection and use of your data
  • We provide secure transmission and storage of any data collected
  • We do not engage in any deceptive, malicious, or harmful practices
